Chapter 1: First-Order Differential Equations

Through my studies, I've organized first-order DEs into three key categories each with distinct solution approaches and real-world applications:

1. Separable Equations

\[ \frac{dy}{dx} = g(x)h(y) \]

My solution approach:

\[ \int \frac{1}{h(y)} \, dy = \int g(x) \, dx + C \]

I applied this to model antibiotic effectiveness in biological systems, where the decay rate depends on current concentration.

2. Exact Equations

\[ M(x,y)dx + N(x,y)dy = 0 \]

I discovered these are crucial in thermodynamics for calculating work done in reversible processes.

3. Linear Equations

\[ \frac{dy}{dx} + P(x)y = Q(x) \]

I used integrating factors to solve circuit analysis problems in my electronics lab.

My Analysis: This interactive graph shows how growth rate (r) affects population dynamics. I observed that r > 0 leads to unsustainable growth, while r < 0 models radioactive decay accurately.

Numerical Methods

Euler's Method in Practice

Euler’s Method approximates solutions using:

yₙ₊₁ = yₙ + h·f(xₙ, yₙ)

While implementing this, I learned that step size choice balances accuracy vs computation cost:

Error ∝ h²
My Finding: Small step sizes (h = 0.01) give smooth approximations but require 10× more computations than h = 1.

Runge-Kutta Method (RK4)

RK4 improves accuracy by combining multiple slope estimates:

k₁ = f(xₙ, yₙ)
k₂ = f(xₙ + h/2, yₙ + (h/2)·k₁)
k₃ = f(xₙ + h/2, yₙ + (h/2)·k₂)
k₄ = f(xₙ + h, yₙ + h·k₃)

yₙ₊₁ = yₙ + (h/6)(k₁ + 2k₂ + 2k₃ + k₄)

Used in your lab code for Newton's Law of Cooling (see Lab section).

Runge-Kutta methods are the backbone of scientific computing.

Linear Differential Equations

General Linear Form

\[ a(x)\frac{dy}{dx} + b(x)y = f(x) \]

Linear DEs appear in circuits, population models, and chemical reactions.

Integrating Factor Method

\[ \mu(x) = e^{\int P(x)dx} \]

Multiply both sides by \(\mu(x)\) to make the equation integrable.

Second-Order Differential Equations

Second-order DEs involve the second derivative and model oscillations, circuits, and mechanical systems. They are essential for understanding vibrations, waves, and stability.

General Linear Form

\[ a\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} + b\frac{dy}{dx} + cy = f(x) \]

Homogeneous Solution

\[ y = C_1 e^{x} + C_2 e^{2x} \]

Describes oscillations in springs, circuits, and more.

Real-Life Applications of Differential Equations

  • Cooling and Heating: Newton’s Law of Cooling models how hot objects cool down (e.g., coffee, forensic science, industrial cooling).
  • Population Growth: Exponential and logistic equations model how populations or bacteria grow and stabilize.
  • Finance: Compound interest, loan amortization, and investment growth are modeled using first-order DEs.
  • Physics: Second-order DEs describe motion (projectiles), spring-mass-damper systems, and electrical circuits (RLC circuits).
  • Medicine: Drug concentration in the bloodstream over time (pharmacokinetics).
  • Engineering: Control systems, signal processing, and vibration analysis all rely on DEs.
  • Chemistry: Reaction rates and radioactive decay are governed by first-order DEs.

Summary: Differential equations are everywhere! From cooling coffee to predicting epidemics, they help us understand and shape the world.
